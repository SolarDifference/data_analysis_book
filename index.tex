\hypertarget{in-the-beginning}{%
\section{In the Beginning\ldots{}}\label{in-the-beginning}}

Though taboo to openly debate with a children's book writer, we think
Seuss (1990) may have gotten it wrong. To be clear, we have no doubt
that learning to code and conduct analytics will take you to many
exciting places that are difficult to foresee. We also think a
stimulating way to spend a casual afternoon involves exploring novel
data sets to see what you discover. However, for those likely to have
picked up this book, any good research or business project (read likely
to be efficiently successful) starts with a clear objective and
well-defined deliverable.

This chapter is about identifying the optimal function of the task at
hand. When working in data analytics (research or industry), identifying
the optimal function involves clearly defining at least two things.
First, what exactly is the thing you are trying to accomplish or learn
by conducting analytics? Second, what is your role on the larger team
involved in the analytic project? The first is applicable to behavior
analysts working in academic research settings and in industry settings.
The second, though important in collaborative research projects, is
likely more applicable to those working in industry settings.
